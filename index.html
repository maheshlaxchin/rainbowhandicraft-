Rainbow Handicraft — Our Story

Empowering women through traditional craftsmanship in Nepal

With more than 20 years of experience in leading different community organizations, our director Mrs. Goma Dhakal started a non-profit organization for children in critical situations called Rainbow Children Home Nepal.

Running the organization for 5 years, her team eventually realized that children often land up in critical situations due to their mothers not being educated or professionally trained. Considering our own traditional skills passed by our ancestors and the opportunities provided by Pokhara developing as the centre of all tourism in Nepal, Mrs. Goma Dhakal founded Rainbow Handicraft Nepal as a small scale industry officially registered under the Department of Small and Cottage Industries.

It took us almost two years to set up the core team, learn all the skills, produce a catalog of unique designs, build quality, and set up a store.

During the early days the VPI Foundation and CIDEN Nepal aided us financially, providing seed funding for the organization to grow. Our long-time friend Mrs. Rosemary Costello dedicated a decade voluntarily for marketing and selling our products across Australia, and Mrs. Marie Kelly also joined in for the beautiful cause.

This provided us more opportunities to train more women. From 12 we grew to more than 45 women producing high quality and exclusive designs of handbags in Pokhara.

During our glory years we were employing 65 women including 12 female prisoners in the district prison, with training centers in 4 villages. Then a 7.6 magnitude mega earthquake struck Nepal, devastating the economy and the tourism market.

We have been rebuilding ever since, developing new strategies including improving our website and trying to expand our international market presence. We have the prayers of the women, the energy and dedication of our director — and we will get through the crisis.

Today, 18 and 19 year old girls from Rainbow Children Home Nepal are the trainers and managers of each department, handling all aspects of production, logistics and quality assurance. Rainbow Handicraft has surely proved a platform for the girls of Rainbow Children Home to hone their skills.

Our Objective

Our Objective

Rainbow Handicraft Udhyog exists to provide sustainable employment and free skill development training to marginalized women in Nepal.

  • Provide free skill development training in weaving, dyeing, tailoring and designing to women in need
  • Generate sustainable income for marginalized women and their families
  • Preserve and promote traditional Nepali handicraft techniques
  • Support the mission of Rainbow Children Home Nepal
  • Build an internationally recognized brand for quality Nepali woven goods

Handicraft History

History of Handicraft in Nepal

Nepal has a rich and ancient tradition of handicraft production dating back thousands of years. The Kathmandu Valley was long a center of artistic production, with artisans creating intricate wood carvings, metalwork, thangka paintings, and woven textiles.

The weaving tradition, in particular, has deep roots across Nepal's different ethnic communities. Dhaka weaving from the eastern hills, Dhangad from western Nepal, and the cotton weaving traditions of the Terai all contribute to a vibrant tapestry of textile craft.

In Pokhara, the growth of tourism over recent decades created new opportunities for handicraft producers. Rainbow Handicraft draws on these deep traditions, training women in skills passed down through generations, and adapting them to produce beautiful, functional bags and home décor items appreciated by visitors from around the world.

Handicraft in Nepal

Handicraft in Nepal

Nepal's handicraft sector is one of the most important export industries in the country, employing hundreds of thousands of people — particularly women in rural and semi-urban areas.

The sector includes products ranging from pashmina shawls and woolen carpets to handmade paper, thangka paintings, silver jewelry, bronze statues, and woven bags. Pokhara, as Nepal's premier tourist destination, is a key hub for handicraft retail and export.

Rainbow Handicraft Udhyog is a proud part of this tradition, contributing both to the economic empowerment of women and to the global appreciation of Nepali craft skills.
